Consistent Shelter

"feeling safe"

Creativity is hard for me without first feeling safe. If things like finances or relationships are not where I want them to be my ability to create slows to a churn if not an outright halt. Without shelter of employment, health, friends, family etc, my desire to create is stunted.

Work

I recently began looking for a new job which completely consumed me and any free time I had. Multiple rounds of interviews often requiring take-home assignments followed by interview panels completely eradicated my attempts to indulge in making art.

When things are calm at work, not being too challenging or easy, my inclination to create begins to flow, but as soon as work shoots in one way or the other my creative output dries up.

Personal

My mother, someone I am not on the best terms with, came to stay recently for knee replacement surgery with the expectation she would only stay for a couple of weeks. Needless to say her visit unraveled into several weeks causing ripples of discontent in my own marriage. Now if my mother and I had a relationship to stand on then I think it would have invigorated my creative process, but without fond memories to pull me up her presence provided zero benefit to me or my family.

The idea of the tortured artist is so foreign to me because during these moments the last thing I would want to do would be to create anything, rather I choose to consume; media, food, alcohol, marijuana or whatever necessary to escape the moment.

Balance

When living my best there seems to be a balance and effortlessness to it all; every puzzle piece seems to just fit, inside and outside. Instead of relying on moments of ease though, I want to use art as a means to lift myself up from the inside when things are down and out on the outside.
